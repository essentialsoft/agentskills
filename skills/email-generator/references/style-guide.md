# Style Guide For Interview Emails

## Tone

- Professional, warm, and concise
- Reassuring and candidate-friendly
- Clear about logistics and expectations

## Writing Rules

- Use short paragraphs
- Prefer concrete wording over vague phrasing
- Keep instructions direct and simple
- Avoid slang and overly casual language

## Content Rules

- Always include the interview purpose
- Always include schedule details or a clear placeholder
- Always include platform instructions (Teams/CoderPad) when relevant
- Include backup communication note when appropriate
- Include signature with sender identity

## Formatting Rules

- Plain text format by default
- Leave one blank line between paragraphs
- Keep meeting details grouped in one block
- Use placeholder tokens in double braces when data is missing

## Final Review

- Candidate name and pronouns are correct
- Date, time, and time zone are unambiguous
- Links are present and readable
- No unresolved placeholders unless explicitly intended
- Closing and signature are complete
