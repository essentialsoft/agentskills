# Role Calibration

Use this reference to tune interview depth, question type, and scoring emphasis based on the target role and seniority.

## Junior Roles

Prioritize:

- problem-solving fundamentals
- debugging approach
- ability to learn quickly
- clarity of thought and communication
- quality-minded engineering habits

De-emphasize:

- broad system design ownership
- executive-level prioritization
- organization-scale architecture strategy

## Mid-Level Roles

Prioritize:

- feature delivery ownership
- tradeoff reasoning
- production debugging and incident handling
- collaboration across functions
- test and quality discipline

Look for evidence that the candidate can move work independently with reasonable guidance.

## Senior Roles

Prioritize:

- architecture judgment
- prioritization under ambiguity
- system reliability and failure handling
- mentoring and technical leadership
- connecting engineering decisions to business outcomes

Look for repeated examples of ownership across ambiguous or high-impact work.

## Staff or Principal Roles

Prioritize:

- cross-team technical strategy
- long-range system thinking
- influence without direct authority
- organizational risk reduction
- balancing short-term delivery against long-term architecture health

Look for evidence of leverage, not just individual contribution.

## Interview Stage Calibration

### Screen

- Confirm baseline fit quickly.
- Focus on core claims, communication, and obvious gaps.
- Use fewer but higher-signal questions.

### Technical Deep Dive

- Probe execution depth, technical judgment, and real examples.
- Use follow-ups to separate ownership from familiarity.

### Panel or Final Loop

- Cover cross-functional fit, prioritization, leadership, and decision quality.
- Ensure the rating matrix reflects final hiring risks, not only technical detail.

## Adjustment Rules

- If the title is senior but the resume evidence is thin, calibrate to the demonstrated level and note the mismatch.
- If the role is domain-heavy, allocate more weight to domain-informed reasoning.
- If the role is execution-heavy, allocate more weight to delivery and problem-solving evidence.
- If the role is leadership-heavy, allocate more weight to influence, prioritization, and communication.
